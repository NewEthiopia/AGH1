APTS-01 THROUGH APTS-05

The Five APTS Result Areas

Every dispensing, procurement, and inventory decision at Adare General Hospital is mapped to one of five result areas defined under the national APTS implementation guideline. Together they form the chain of custody between budget allocation and the medicine handed to a patient.

APTS-01

Financial & Transactional Transparency

Standardized vouchers govern every cash, credit, and free-service transaction. Daily sales are reconciled against the cashier's register before close of business, producing an auditable trail from prescription to payment.

APTS-02

Scientific Budget Utilization

Consumption data is reconciled through ABC/VEN cross-analysis, ensuring vital, high-cost, and high-volume medicines are prioritized in the procurement plan ahead of lower-priority items.

APTS-03

Workforce Deployment

Staffing is allocated against measured workload-to-staff ratios at each dispensing point, eliminating queue bottlenecks during peak OPD and inpatient discharge hours.

APTS-04

Inventory Management

Systematic physical inventory counts, bin-card reconciliation, and stock-status classification (overstock, understock, expiring) keep shelf reality aligned with recorded balances.

APTS-05

Quality Patient Care

Counseling-time metrics, labeling accuracy, and last-mile delivery to inpatient wards are tracked together to ensure clinical instructions reach the patient intact and on time.

"Every medicine dispensed at public expense must be traceable, in full, from procurement order to patient record."

SECTION 04

Tools & Analysis

Working instruments used by pharmacy and finance staff to convert daily transaction data into decisions about procurement, staffing, and loss prevention.

Open the analysis workspace

ABC/VEN Reconciliation Worksheet

Cross-tabulates annual consumption value against vitality classification to flag misallocated spend.

Stock Status Calculator

Classifies each item as overstocked, adequately stocked, understocked, or stocked-out against monthly consumption average.

Daily Sales & Cash Reconciliation Sheet

Matches dispensed-item value against register cash, credit notes, and free-service vouchers at end of shift.

Wastage & Expiry Tracker

Logs near-expiry batches by FEFO order and calculates loss value against the quarterly wastage ceiling.

Workload-to-Staff Ratio Sheet

Measures prescriptions-per-dispenser-hour by unit to support staffing redeployment decisions.

Bin Card & Physical Count Log

Standard template for periodic physical inventory counts and variance notation against bin card balances.

SECTION 05

Operational Units Directory

Each pharmacy unit at Adare General Hospital operates under the same APTS register and voucher standard, adapted to the clinical tempo and patient flow specific to that point of care.

Emergency Pharmacy

24-hour dispensing point serving the emergency department, operating on expedited voucher clearance for acute cases.

Outpatient (OPD) Dispensary

Highest-volume unit by prescription count; primary site for workload-to-staff ratio monitoring and counseling-time tracking.

Inpatient Store

Ward-level stock holding and unit-dose distribution; reconciled against bin cards during weekly physical counts.

Chronic / ART Care Pharmacy

Dedicated refill scheduling for chronic-disease and antiretroviral therapy patients, with adherence and stock-out alerting.
